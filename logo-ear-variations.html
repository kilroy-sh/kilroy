The Ear — Variations

Exploring the Hearsay ear mark across stroke weight, fill style, geometry, and abstraction level. Each variant includes an ASCII rendering for terminal interfaces.

Concentric Arcs

Pure geometric half-circles — the ear reduced to its mathematical essence. Doubles as a signal/broadcast icon. The ASCII version is incredibly clean: ●))). Best TUI candidate by far.

SVG
╭────╮ ╭╯╭──╯│ │╭╯ ○ │ │╰──╯╭─╯ ╰───╯
ASCII

Spiral

A continuous spiraling path — knowledge winding inward toward a center point. Evokes the cochlea. Beautiful but more complex; the single continuous stroke has an elegant, meditative quality.

Category 4

Abstraction Level

From literal anatomy to pure symbol.

SVG
╭───╮ ╭╯╭─╮│ │╭╯╭╮││ │├●╰╯│╯ │╰──╯│ ╰╮╭╯ ╰╯
ASCII

Anatomical

Close to a real ear's structure — helix, antihelix, tragus, concha. Rich and detailed. Risks feeling "medical" but anchors the metaphor firmly. More illustration than logo.

SVG
╭──╮ │╭╮│ │● │ ╰──╯
ASCII
Recommended

Minimal

Two curves and a dot. That's it. Reads as "ear" without trying too hard. Clean enough for a favicon, distinct enough to be ownable. The less you show, the more memorable it becomes.

SVG
╮ │ ●│ │ ╯
ASCII

Ultra-Abstract

A single curve and a dot — a closing parenthesis with a period. The absolute minimum needed to suggest "listening." Almost typographic. ASCII version: literally ●).
