The Whisper Pair

Two figures. One's leaning in. That's gossip. That's hearsay. Every variation below explores how abstract or human that pair can be.

The Concept

One figure whispering to another

The single most recognizable visual for gossip across all cultures. Reduced to its essence: two shapes, one tilted.

o bg O /|··· |\ / \ / \
The vibe

The whisperer leans in (tilted ~12°). Tiny dots float between them — the secret being passed. The listener stands upright, slightly larger (receiving). This is the seed. Everything below is a variation of this moment.

Just Heads

Two circles, one tilted and smaller (leaning in), one upright and larger (listening). Tiny dots between. Reduces to o··O in a terminal. Dead simple, still reads as gossip.

The Overlap

Two circles overlapping — the shared zone IS the hearsay. Venn-diagram energy. Minimal and clever, but might lose the "gossip" read without the tilt. More "collaboration" than "secret."

Parentheses

A tilted ( whispering to an upright ). Typographic, witty, extremely ownable. The left paren leans in at 14°. In monospace: (··). Lives natively in code and terminal.

Dots & Trail

A small dot, a trail of whisper particles, a big dot. That's it. The size difference implies direction — info flowing from source to receiver. TUI: o···O. Five characters.

Detail Study

What's Between Them?

The space between the two figures matters. These are different ways to render "the whisper" itself.

Fading Dots

Decreasing size and opacity. The whisper dissipates as it travels. Delicate, ephemeral — gossip that might not survive the journey.

Recommended

Nothing — Just Proximity

No dots, no lines. The lean and closeness do all the work. The empty space IS the whisper. Cleaner, bolder, more confident. The gossip is implied.

Ghost Bubble

A barely-there speech bubble floating between them. The gossip is almost invisible — you have to look for it. Adds a layer of meaning but might overcomplicate the mark.
